<br>

### Thesis
### <i>For the Optimal Structuring of Non-Linear Short-Term Forecast Models of the Energy Output of a Small Wind Farm</i>

<br>

<img src="https://github.com/sitdisch/cloud/blob/master/gifs/m_thesis.gif?raw=true" width="800"/>

</div>

> <b>**Note**</b>: Due to non-disclosure clauses, only fake data is shown.

#### Research Framework & Data Preprocessing
> * <b>Dependent Variable</b>: energy output of a small onshore wind farm in a rural region
> 
> * <b>Potential Features</b>: historical energy output and meteorological data from distributed weather stations
> * <b>Forecast Horizon</b>: short-term (6h)
> * <b>Forecast Approach</b>: multi-stage prediction
> * <b>Data Preprocessing</b>: time series analysis, plausibility checks, data cleansing  and normalization
> * <b>Data Division</b>: Training, validation and testing data were randomly selected (considering seasonality)

#### Feature Selection, Model Approaches, Adaptation & Validation
> * <b>Feature Selection</b>: automatic searches with complete, unbalanced, one-dimensional N-Way-ANOVAs with fixed effects, nominally scaled factors and without covariates
> 
> * <b>Model Approaches</b>: persistence model, ARX models and neural networks
> * <b>Weigth Adaptation</b>: epochal learning with Levenberg-Marquardt backpropagation algorithm
> * <b>Architectural Adaptation</b>: grid search for the number of hidden layers, number of hidden neurons, and type of activation functions
> *	<b>Initialization Algorithm</b>: Nguyen Widrow
> *	<b>Regularization Technique</b>: early stopping
> * <b>Model Validation</b>: qualitative, quantitative (e.&nbsp;g. R<sup>2</sup>, RMSE, NMAE) and residual distribution analysis

<br>

### Term Paper
### <i>Overview: Evolutionary Artificial Neural Networks</i>

<br>

<img src="https://github.com/sitdisch/cloud/blob/master/gifs/eann.gif?raw=true" width="800"/>

</div>

> * <b>Connection Weights</b>: purely evolutionary or with hybrid strategies
> 
> * <b>Network Architectures</b>: purely evolutionary or combined with con-/ destructive algorithms
> * <b>Learning Algorithms</b>: with variable or fixed structure
> * <b>Input Data</b>: feature selection, data division, development of new examples

#### Use of:
> * <b>Genetic Algorithms</b> and <b>Genetic Programming</b>, <b>Coevolution</b>, <b>Evolutionary Strategies</b> and <b>Evolutionary Programming</b>
> 
> * <b>Genetic Operators</b>: recombination, permutation, mutation...
> * <b>Genotypic Representations</b>: architectures, weights, activation functions, learning rules, features...
> * <b>Weights Coding</b>: binary, real, decimal and mixed
> * <b>Architectures Coding</b>: direct or indirect (e.&nbsp;g. Michigan or Pitt approach)
> * <b>Niche Building</b>: fitness sharing, local or regional population models

> * <b>scenario simulations</b> based on measurement datasets
> * <b>eco-efficiency analysis</b> of the scenarios and <b>error estimation</b>

### Project Study
### <i>Extension of the Database for the Ecological and Economic Evaluation of Small Plants for the Co-Combustion of Municipal Waste</i>

<br>

<img src="https://github.com/sitdisch/cloud/blob/master/gifs/project_studies.gif?raw=true" width="800"/>

</div>

> <b>**Note**</b>: Due to non-disclosure clauses, only fake data is shown.

#### Planning & Execution of Co-Combustion Trials
>	* development of <b>co-combustion scenarios</b>; taking into account fuel-related properties as well as composition and quantity of municipal waste
>	
>	* trial series <b>planning</b> as well as <b>procurement</b> and <b>preparation</b> of the secondary fuel
>	* <b>execution</b> and <b>monitoring</b> of the trials on a combustion plant in a technical research center
> * measurement: <b>T<sub>emp</sub></b>, <b>P<sub>res</sub></b>, <b>O<sub>2</sub></b>, <b>CO<sub>2</sub></b>, <b>CO</b>, <b>NO<sub>2</sub></b>, <b>HCl</b>, <b>SO<sub>2</sub></b> and <b>dust concentrations</b>
> * ash processing and <b>determination</b> of <b>loss on ignition</b>
> * <b>documentation</b>, <b>conversion</b>, <b>evaluation</b> and <b>interpretation</b> of the data
> * calculation of the <b>flue gas quantities</b> and the <b>energy balance</b> of the fuels used

#### Compilation & Execution of an Eco-Efficiency Analysis
>	* definition of <b>objectives</b>, selection of <b>comparison systems</b> and delimitation of the <b>research framework</b>
>	
>	* <b>Life Cycle Assessments</b> (ISO&nbsp;14040 and ISO&nbsp;14044)
>	* <b>Total Cost</b> of <b>Ownership</b> (TCO) calculations
>	* <b>weighting</b>, <b>aggregation</b> and <b>normalization</b> of the data
>	* system comparison in an <b>eco-efficiency</b> result portfolio
>	* result <b>evaluation</b>, <b>sensitivity analysis</b> and <b>error estimation</b>
